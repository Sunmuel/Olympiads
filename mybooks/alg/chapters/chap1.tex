\section{\textsf{Replacements}}
\subsection{\textsf{abc=1}}
If $abc=1$, replacing $a$, $b$, $c$ with $\frac{x}{y}$, $\frac{y}{z}$,
$\frac{z}{x}$, respectively, usually homogenizes the inequality.
\subsection{\textsf{Ravi substitution}}
If $a$,$b$,$c$ are said to be the sides of a triangle, replace them with
$y+z$, $z+x$,$x+y$.

\section{\textsf{Schur's Inequality}}
Let $a$,$b$,$c$ be nonnegative real numbers and $r>0$ be positive real
number. Then

\[
	\cyc a^r(a^2+bc) \ge \cyc a^{r+1}(b+c).
\]

Equality occurs if $a=b=c$ or $a=b$ and $c=0$ and permutations.

For example, when $r=1$:

\[
	\cyc (a^3+abc) \ge \cyc a^2b.
\]

An easier way to write the Schur's Inequality is the following:

\[
	\cyc a^r(a-b)(a-c) \ge 0.
\]
